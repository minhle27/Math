\begin{problem}{}{}{A Walk Through Combinatorics - Ch.9 Ex.1 p.225}
    Let $G$ be a loopless undirected graph. Prove that the edges of $G$ 
    can be directed so that no directed cycle is formed.
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}
Let $G$ be a graph on $n$ vertices. We need to show the 
edges of $G$ can be directed in such a way that no directed cycle 
is formed. Let's label vertices from 1 to $n$. 

First, make every of $n-1$ edges connected to 1 be an out edge. 
It follows that no possible directed cycle can start from 1. 

Now, consider all remaining $n-2$ undirected edges connected to 2 
and make each of them to be an out edge. It also follows that no 
directed cycle can start from 2. 

Similarly, continue with this labelling scheme, no directed cycle 
can start from any of the vertices.
\end{solution}
