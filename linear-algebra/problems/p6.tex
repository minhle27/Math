\begin{problem}{}{}{Linear Algebra Done Right - Ex.18 p.67}
Suppose \(V\) and \(W\) are finite-dimensional and that \(U\) is a subspace
of \(V\). Prove that there exists \(T \in \mathcal{L}(V, W)\) such that
\(\operatorname{null} T = U\) if and only if
\(
\dim U \ge \dim V - \dim W.
\)
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}
($\Rightarrow$) Suppose there exists $T \in \mathcal{L}(V,W)$ such that $\text{null } T = U$. 
By the fundamental theorem of linear maps, we have:
\[ \dim V = \dim \text{null } T + \dim \text{range } T \]
\[ \Rightarrow \dim U = \dim V - \dim \text{range } T \ge \dim V - \dim W \]

($\Leftarrow$) Suppose $U$ is a subspace of $V$ such that $\dim U \ge \dim V - \dim W$.
Let $u_1, u_2, \dots, u_n$ be a basis of $U$. 
This list can be extended to $u_1, u_2, \dots, u_n, v_1, v_2, \dots, v_m$ 
to be a basis of $V \Rightarrow \dim V = m + n$.

We have $\dim U \ge \dim V - \dim W \Rightarrow \dim W \ge \dim V - \dim U = m$.
Therefore, we can let $w_1, w_2, \dots, w_m$ be an independent list of vectors in $W$.
Define $T: V \to W$ as:
\[ T(c_1u_1 + \dots + c_nu_n + d_1v_1 + \dots + d_mv_m) = d_1w_1 + d_2w_2 + \dots + d_mw_m \]

It is easy to verify that $T$ is a linear map by checking $T(u+v) = T(u)+T(v)$ 
and $T(\lambda u) = \lambda T(u)$, $\forall u,v \in V$.

We have for all $u \in U$, 
$Tu = T(c_1u_1 + \dots + c_nu_n) = 0w_1 + \dots + 0w_m = 0$
$\Rightarrow u \in \text{null } T \Rightarrow U \subseteq \text{null } T$.

Now, let $u \in \text{null } T$. We have $Tu = 0$
$\Rightarrow d_1 = d_2 = \dots = d_m = 0$ since $w_1, w_2, \dots, w_m$ are independent.
Thus, $u$ can be expressed as $c_1u_1 + c_2u_2 + \dots + c_nu_n$ 
$\Rightarrow u \in U \Rightarrow \text{null } T \subseteq U$.

Therefore, $U = \text{null } T$.
\end{solution}
