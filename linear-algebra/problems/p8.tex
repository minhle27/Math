\begin{problem}{}{}{Linear Algebra Done Right - Ex.5 p.79}
Suppose $V$ and $W$ are finite-dimensional and $T \in \mathcal{L}(V, W)$. Prove that there
exist a basis of $V$ and a basis of $W$ such that with respect to these bases, all
entries of $\mathcal{M}(T)$ are $0$ except that the entries in row $k$, column $k$, equal $1$ if
$1 \leq k \leq \dim \text{range } T$.
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}
By Thm 3.21: $\dim V = \dim \text{null } T + \dim \text{range } T$

Let $m = \dim \text{null } T = \dim V - \dim \text{range } T$

Let $u_1, u_2, \dots, u_m$ be a basis of $\text{null } T$. 
Since $\text{null } T$ is a subspace of $V$, this list can be 
extended to $v_1, v_2, \dots, v_n, u_1, \dots, u_m$ to be a basis of $V$. 
This implies $\dim V = m + n$ and $\dim \text{range } T = n$.

Consider $a_1 T v_1 + a_2 T v_2 + \dots + a_n T v_n = 0$.
\begin{align*}
    &\Rightarrow T(a_1 v_1 + a_2 v_2 + \dots + a_n v_n) = 0 \\
    &\Rightarrow a_1 v_1 + \dots + a_n v_n \in \text{null } T \\
    &\Rightarrow a_1 v_1 + \dots + a_n v_n = b_1 u_1 + \dots + b_m u_m \\
    &\Rightarrow a_1 = a_2 = \dots = a_n = 0 \text{ since } v_1, v_2, \dots, v_n, u_1, \dots, u_m \text{ are independent}
\end{align*}

This means $T v_1, T v_2, \dots, T v_n$ are independent in $W$ and 
this list can be extended to become a basis of $W$. 
With this construction of a basis of $V$ and a basis of $W$, 
$\mathcal{M}(T)$ will have the desired entries.
\end{solution}
